Notes - Chapter 5: Arithmetic Progressions

Introduction to Arithmetic Progressions (AP)

An arithmetic progression (AP) is a sequence of numbers in which the difference between consecutive terms is constant. This difference is called the common difference and is denoted by d. The general form of an arithmetic progression is:

a, a+d, a+2d, a+3d, ...

Where:

  • a is the first term of the AP.
  • d is the common difference between consecutive terms.

Formulas for Arithmetic Progressions

  • nth term of AP: The nth term of an arithmetic progression is given by the formula: 
    an = a + (n - 1) * d
  • Sum of first n terms: The sum of the first n terms of an arithmetic progression is given by: 
    Sn = (n / 2) * [2a + (n - 1) * d]
  • Sum of n terms (alternative form): The sum of the first n terms can also be written as: 
    Sn = (n / 2) * (a + l)
    Where l is the last term of the sequence.

Types of Problems in AP

Some common types of problems that involve arithmetic progressions include:

  • Finding the nth term of an AP.
  • Finding the sum of the first n terms of an AP.
  • Identifying the common difference.
  • Solving real-life problems related to arithmetic progressions (e.g., distance, time, and money).

Examples

Consider the arithmetic progression: 2, 5, 8, 11, 14...

  • First term, a = 2
  • Common difference, d = 3

The 10th term can be calculated as:

a10 = 2 + (10 - 1) * 3 = 2 + 27 = 29

Important Theorems and Properties

  • Arithmetic Mean: The arithmetic mean between two numbers is the average of those numbers, and it is also the midpoint of the arithmetic progression containing those numbers.
  • Equal Differences: In an arithmetic progression, the difference between any two consecutive terms is always constant.

Applications of Arithmetic Progressions

Arithmetic progressions have various applications, such as:

  • In calculating interest (e.g., simple interest).
  • In construction (e.g., staircases or ramps).
  • In solving problems related to time, distance, and money (e.g., savings, payments).
