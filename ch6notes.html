Notes - Chapter 6: Triangles

Introduction to Triangles

A triangle is a polygon with three sides and three angles. The sum of the interior angles of any triangle is always 180°. Triangles are classified based on their sides and angles. The main types of triangles are:

  • Equilateral Triangle: All three sides are equal, and all three angles are 60°.
  • Isosceles Triangle: Two sides are equal, and the angles opposite these sides are equal.
  • Scalene Triangle: All sides and angles are different.

Important Properties of Triangles

  • Angle Sum Property: The sum of the three interior angles of a triangle is 180°.
  • Exterior Angle Theorem: The exterior angle of a triangle is equal to the sum of the two opposite interior angles.
  • Congruence of Triangles: Two triangles are congruent if their corresponding sides and angles are equal.
  • Similarity of Triangles: Two triangles are similar if their corresponding angles are equal, and the lengths of corresponding sides are proportional.

Types of Problems in Triangles

Some common types of problems that involve triangles include:

  • Finding the unknown sides or angles of a triangle using trigonometric ratios.
  • Using the Pythagorean theorem in right-angled triangles.
  • Solving problems based on the properties of congruent or similar triangles.
  • Finding areas of triangles using various formulas.

Examples

Consider a right-angled triangle where the base is 3 cm and the height is 4 cm. To find the hypotenuse, we can use the Pythagorean theorem:

c² = a² + b²

Where a = 3 cm and b = 4 cm.

Thus, c² = 3² + 4² = 9 + 16 = 25, so c = 5 cm is the length of the hypotenuse.

Important Theorems and Formulas

  • Pythagorean Theorem: For a right-angled triangle, c² = a² + b², where c is the hypotenuse.
  • Area of a Triangle: The area of a triangle is given by the formula: 
    A = 1/2 * base * height
  • Sine Rule: 
    a/sin(A) = b/sin(B) = c/sin(C)
  • Cosine Rule: 
    c² = a² + b² - 2ab * cos(C)

Applications of Triangles

Triangles have several real-world applications, such as:

  • In construction and engineering (e.g., trusses, bridges, roofs).
  • In navigation and mapping (e.g., triangulation).
  • In computer graphics and design (e.g., modeling 3D shapes using triangles).
