Notes - Chapter 9: Light – Reflection and Refraction

Introduction

Light is a form of energy that enables us to see the world around us. This chapter explores two important properties of light: reflection and refraction.

Reflection of Light

  • Reflection: When light rays bounce off a surface, it is called reflection.
  • Law of Reflection: The angle of incidence is equal to the angle of reflection.
  • Types of Reflection: Regular reflection (from smooth surfaces) and diffused reflection (from rough surfaces).
  • Applications: Mirrors, periscopes, and reflecting telescopes.

Refraction of Light

  • Refraction: Bending of light as it passes from one medium to another.
  • Laws of Refraction: The incident ray, refracted ray, and normal lie in the same plane; Snell’s Law.
  • Causes of Refraction: Change in speed of light in different media.
  • Examples: Bending of a pencil in water, formation of a rainbow.

Lenses

  • Convex Lenses: Converge light rays; used in magnifying glasses and cameras.
  • Concave Lenses: Diverge light rays; used in eyeglasses for myopia.

Important Concepts

  • Real and virtual images formed by mirrors and lenses.
  • Focus, focal length, and centre of curvature.
