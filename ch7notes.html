Notes - Chapter 7: Coordinate Geometry

Introduction to Coordinate Geometry

Coordinate geometry is the branch of geometry where the position of points on the plane is described using ordered pairs of numbers (coordinates). The fundamental concept of coordinate geometry is the Cartesian coordinate system, introduced by René Descartes.

Key topics in coordinate geometry include:

  • Cartesian Plane: A two-dimensional plane consisting of an x-axis (horizontal) and a y-axis (vertical).
  • Distance Formula: The formula used to calculate the distance between two points.
  • Midpoint Formula: The formula used to find the midpoint between two points.
  • Slope of a Line: The measure of the steepness of a line.
  • Equation of a Line: Mathematical representation of a straight line.

Important Formulas and Theorems

  • Distance Formula: To find the distance between two points (x₁, y₁) and (x₂, y₂), use the formula: 
    d = √[(x₂ - x₁)² + (y₂ - y₁)²]
  • Midpoint Formula: To find the midpoint between two points (x₁, y₁) and (x₂, y₂), use the formula: 
    Midpoint = ((x₁ + x₂)/2, (y₁ + y₂)/2)
  • Slope of a Line: The slope of a line passing through the points (x₁, y₁) and (x₂, y₂) is: 
    m = (y₂ - y₁) / (x₂ - x₁)
  • Equation of a Line: The equation of a line in slope-intercept form is: 
    y = mx + b
    Where m is the slope and b is the y-intercept.

Applications of Coordinate Geometry

Coordinate geometry has various real-world applications, such as:

  • Finding the location of points in computer graphics and design.
  • Calculating distances in navigation and mapping.
  • Solving problems related to the geometry of shapes and lines.
