Notes - Chapter 5: Life Processes

Introduction

Life processes are the essential functions that sustain life in all living organisms. This chapter covers the processes like nutrition, respiration, transportation, and excretion which help organisms maintain their life activities.

Nutrition

  • Autotrophic Nutrition: Organisms like plants prepare their own food through photosynthesis.
  • Heterotrophic Nutrition: Organisms like animals depend on other organisms for food.
  • Types of Nutrition: Herbivores, carnivores, omnivores based on diet.

Respiration

  • Process of breaking down food to release energy.
  • Types: Aerobic (requires oxygen) and anaerobic (without oxygen).
  • Respiration in humans involves lungs and blood circulation.

Transportation

  • Transport of nutrients, gases, and wastes in plants and animals.
  • In plants: xylem transports water, phloem transports food.
  • In animals: circulatory system transports blood and nutrients.

Excretion

  • Removal of metabolic waste products from the body.
  • In humans, kidneys filter waste; plants release gases through stomata.
  • Maintains internal chemical balance and homeostasis.
