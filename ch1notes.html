Notes - Chapter 1: Real Numbers

Introduction to Real Numbers

Real numbers include all the rational and irrational numbers. They can be expressed as a decimal or a fraction...

Types of Real Numbers

  • Rational Numbers: Numbers written as a fraction. Ex: 1/2, 3/4
  • Irrational Numbers: Can't be expressed as a fraction. Ex: √2, π

Properties of Real Numbers

  • Closure: Sum/product of real numbers is real.
  • Commutative: a + b = b + a
  • Associative: (a + b) + c = a + (b + c)

Operations on Real Numbers

They can be added, subtracted, multiplied, divided, and raised to powers.

Examples and Applications

- 5 + (-3) = 2
- 4 × (-2) = -8
- x + 3 = 7 → x = 4

Important Definitions

- Rational: ratio of integers
- Irrational: not ratio of integers
- Integer: whole number, can be negative
