Notes - Chapter 1: Real Numbers

Introduction to Real Numbers

Real numbers include all the rational and irrational numbers. They can be expressed as a decimal or a fraction. The real number system consists of natural numbers, whole numbers, integers, rational numbers, and irrational numbers. Real numbers can be plotted on a number line, and they have properties such as closure, commutative, and associative properties for addition and multiplication.

Types of Real Numbers

Real numbers are divided into two broad categories:

  • Rational Numbers: Numbers that can be written as a fraction of two integers. Examples: 1/2, 3/4, -7/8.
  • Irrational Numbers: Numbers that cannot be expressed as fractions. Examples: √2, π.

Properties of Real Numbers

Real numbers have several important properties, such as:

  • Closure Property: The sum or product of any two real numbers is also a real number.
  • Commutative Property: The order of addition or multiplication does not change the result. Example: a + b = b + a.
  • Associative Property: The grouping of numbers does not affect the sum or product. Example: (a + b) + c = a + (b + c).

Operations on Real Numbers

Real numbers can undergo various operations such as addition, subtraction, multiplication, division, and exponentiation. Understanding how to operate on real numbers is essential for solving equations and inequalities.

Examples and Applications

- Adding real numbers: 5 + (-3) = 2
- Multiplying real numbers: 4 * (-2) = -8
- Solving real number equations: x + 3 = 7 → x = 4

Important Definitions

- Rational Numbers: Numbers that can be written as a ratio of two integers.
- Irrational Numbers: Numbers that cannot be written as a ratio of two integers.
- Integer: A number without a decimal or fraction part, can be positive, negative, or zero.
