Notes - Chapter 4: Carbon and its Compounds

Introduction

Carbon is a unique element known for its ability to form a vast number of compounds due to its tetravalency and catenation property. This chapter explores the nature, properties, and compounds of carbon.

Allotropes of Carbon

  • Diamond: Hardest natural substance, transparent, and an electrical insulator.
  • Graphite: Soft, slippery, conducts electricity, and used as a lubricant.
  • Fullerenes: Molecules made entirely of carbon in the form of hollow spheres, ellipsoids, or tubes.

Properties of Carbon Compounds

  • Typically covalent compounds with low melting and boiling points.
  • Mostly insoluble in water but soluble in organic solvents.
  • Exhibit isomerism.
  • Undergo combustion and substitution reactions.

Important Carbon Compounds

  • Hydrocarbons: Compounds containing only carbon and hydrogen.
  • Alcohols: Organic compounds containing -OH group (e.g., ethanol).
  • Acids: Organic acids like acetic acid (vinegar).
  • Esters: Responsible for pleasant smells and flavors.

Uses of Carbon Compounds

  • Fuel (petrol, diesel, natural gas).
  • Plastics and synthetic materials.
  • Pharmaceuticals and medicines.
  • Food preservatives and flavoring agents.
