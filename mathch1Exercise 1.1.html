Exercise 1.1 Solution - Real Numbers

Question 1: Express each number as a product of its prime factors:

  • (i) 140

    • Step 1: Divide by 2: 140 ÷ 2 = 70

    Step 2: Divide by 2 again: 70 ÷ 2 = 35

    Step 3: Divide by 5: 35 ÷ 5 = 7

    Step 4: Divide by 7: 7 ÷ 7 = 1

    Prime Factorization of 140: 22 × 5 × 7

  • (ii) 156

    • Step 1: Divide by 2: 156 ÷ 2 = 78

    Step 2: Divide by 2 again: 78 ÷ 2 = 39

    Step 3: Divide by 3: 39 ÷ 3 = 13

    Step 4: Divide by 13: 13 ÷ 13 = 1

    Prime Factorization of 156: 22 × 3 × 13

  • (iii) 3825

    • Step 1: Divide by 5: 3825 ÷ 5 = 765

    Step 2: Divide by 5 again: 765 ÷ 5 = 153

    Step 3: Divide by 3: 153 ÷ 3 = 51

    Step 4: Divide by 3 again: 51 ÷ 3 = 17

    Step 5: Divide by 17: 17 ÷ 17 = 1

    Prime Factorization of 3825: 52 × 32 × 17

  • (iv) 5005

    • Step 1: Divide by 5: 5005 ÷ 5 = 1001

    Step 2: Divide by 7: 1001 ÷ 7 = 143

    Step 3: Divide by 11: 143 ÷ 11 = 13

    Step 4: Divide by 13: 13 ÷ 13 = 1

    Prime Factorization of 5005: 5 × 7 × 11 × 13

  • (v) 7429

    • Step 1: Divide by 7: 7429 ÷ 7 = 1062.71 (non-integer)

    Step 2: Divide by 11: 7429 ÷ 11 = 675.36 (non-integer)

    Step 3: Check divisibility by 13: 7429 ÷ 13 = 571 (integer)

    Step 4: 571 is a prime number.

    Prime Factorization of 7429: 13 × 571

Question 2: Find the LCM and HCF of the following pairs of integers and verify that LCM × HCF = product of the two numbers.

  • (i) 26 and 91

    • Prime factorization of 26: 2 × 13

    Prime factorization of 91: 7 × 13

    HCF = 13 (common factor)

    LCM = 2 × 7 × 13 = 182

    Verification: LCM × HCF = 182 × 13 = 2366 = 26 × 91

  • (ii) 510 and 92

    • Prime factorization of 510: 2 × 3 × 5 × 17

    Prime factorization of 92: 22 × 23

    HCF = 2

    LCM = 22 × 3 × 5 × 17 × 23 = 23,220

    Verification: LCM × HCF = 23,220 × 2 = 46,440 = 510 × 92

Question 3: Find the LCM and HCF of the following integers by applying the prime factorisation method.

  • (i) 12, 15, and 21

    • Prime factorization of 12: 22 × 3

    Prime factorization of 15: 3 × 5

    Prime factorization of 21: 3 × 7

    HCF = 3 (common factor)

    LCM = 22 × 3 × 5 × 7 = 420

Question 4: Given that HCF (306, 657) = 9, find LCM (306, 657).

Solution:

We know the relationship between HCF and LCM is: LCM × HCF = Product of the numbers

LCM = (306 × 657) ÷ HCF

LCM = (306 × 657) ÷ 9 = 20022 ÷ 9 = 2224

LCM of 306 and 657: 2224

Question 5: Check whether 6n can end with the digit 0 for any natural number n.

Solution:

For a number to end with 0, it must be divisible by both 2 and 5. But 6n is divisible by 2 but not necessarily by 5.

Therefore, 6n cannot end with the digit 0 for any natural number n.

Question 6: Explain why 7 × 11 × 13 + 13 and 7 × 6 × 5 × 4 × 3 × 2 × 1 + 5 are composite numbers.

Solution:

For the first number: 7 × 11 × 13 + 13 = 1001 + 13 = 1014, which is divisible by 2, so it is composite.

For the second number: 7 × 6 × 5 × 4 × 3 × 2 × 1 + 5 = 5040 + 5 = 5045, which is divisible by 5, so it is composite.
