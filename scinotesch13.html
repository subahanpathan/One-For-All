Notes - Chapter 13: Our Environment

Introduction

Our environment includes all living and non-living things occurring naturally on Earth. It provides us with resources like air, water, and soil necessary for survival.

Components of the Environment

  • Biotic Components: All living organisms like plants, animals, and microbes.
  • Abiotic Components: Non-living elements such as air, water, soil, and sunlight.

Types of Environment

  • Natural Environment: Includes forests, oceans, rivers, and mountains.
  • Built Environment: Human-made surroundings like cities, roads, and buildings.

Environmental Issues

  • Pollution: Contamination of air, water, and soil affecting living organisms.
  • Deforestation: Cutting down forests causing habitat loss and climate change.
  • Climate Change: Changes in global or regional climate patterns due to human activities.
  • Waste Management: Proper disposal and recycling of waste to reduce pollution.

Conservation of Environment

  • Reduce, Reuse, Recycle: Minimizing waste and promoting sustainable use of resources.
  • Afforestation: Planting trees to restore forests and maintain ecological balance.
  • Use of Renewable Energy: Solar, wind, and hydro energy to reduce pollution.
  • Water Conservation: Saving water through efficient use and preventing wastage.

Role of Individuals

Each person can contribute to protecting the environment by adopting eco-friendly habits, spreading awareness, and participating in environmental activities.
