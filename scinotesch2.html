Chapter 2: Acids, Bases and Salts - Notes

Introduction

This chapter discusses the nature and properties of acids, bases, and salts. It covers how these substances react with each other and their importance in everyday life.

Acids

Acids are substances that release H⁺ ions in aqueous solution. Common properties include:

  • Sour taste
  • Turn blue litmus red
  • React with metals to release hydrogen gas
  • Conduct electricity in aqueous solution

Bases

Bases are substances that release OH⁻ ions in aqueous solution. Properties include:

  • Bitter taste
  • Slippery to touch
  • Turn red litmus blue
  • React with acids to form salt and water

Salts

Salts are formed when acids react with bases in a neutralization reaction. Examples:

  • NaCl (table salt) from HCl and NaOH
  • CuSO₄ from H₂SO₄ and CuO

Importance in Daily Life

  • Hydrochloric acid in the stomach aids digestion.
  • NaOH used in soap making.
  • Baking soda (NaHCO₃) used in cooking and as an antacid.

Indicators

Indicators are substances that change color in acidic or basic solutions:

  • Litmus: Blue in base, red in acid
  • Phenolphthalein: Colorless in acid, pink in base
  • Methyl orange: Red in acid, yellow in base

Conclusion

Acids, bases, and salts are essential in both laboratory and daily applications. Understanding their properties and reactions is vital for deeper study in chemistry.
