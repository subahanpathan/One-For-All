Notes - Chapter 11: Electricity

Introduction

Electricity is a fundamental part of our daily lives. This chapter explores the nature of electric current, circuits, conductors, insulators, and the laws governing electricity.

Electric Current

  • Definition: The flow of electric charge through a conductor is called electric current.
  • Unit: The unit of current is Ampere (A).
  • Conventional Current: Flow of positive charge from positive to negative terminal.
  • Electron Flow: Actual flow of electrons from negative to positive terminal.

Electric Circuits

  • Components: Battery, wires, switch, resistor (load).
  • Types of Circuits: Series circuit and parallel circuit.
  • Switch: Used to open or close a circuit.

Conductors and Insulators

  • Conductors: Materials that allow electric current to flow easily (e.g., copper, aluminum).
  • Insulators: Materials that do not allow electric current to flow easily (e.g., rubber, plastic).

Ohm's Law

  • States that the current through a conductor between two points is directly proportional to the voltage across the two points, provided the temperature remains constant.
  • Mathematically, V = IR, where V is voltage, I is current, and R is resistance.

Electric Power and Energy

  • Electric Power: Rate at which electrical energy is consumed or produced; P = VI.
  • Units: Power in Watts (W), Energy in Joules (J) or Kilowatt-hour (kWh).

Safety Measures

  • Use of fuses and circuit breakers to prevent overload.
  • Proper insulation of wires.
  • Avoiding wet hands while handling electrical devices.
