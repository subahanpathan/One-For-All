Chapter 9: Some Applications of Trigonometry - Notes

Introduction

Trigonometry has numerous practical applications in fields such as architecture, navigation, and engineering. It helps in measuring angles, heights, distances, and solving real-world problems involving triangles.

Applications of Trigonometry

Trigonometry is used in a wide variety of fields, including:

  • Architecture: Trigonometry helps architects to determine angles and dimensions when designing buildings and structures.
  • Navigation: Trigonometry is essential in navigation to calculate distances and angles between locations, especially in air and sea navigation.
  • Engineering: Engineers use trigonometry to design machines, bridges, and other structures, ensuring stability and correct measurements.
  • Surveying: Trigonometry is used in land surveying to measure distances, areas, and angles between landmarks.
  • Physics: Trigonometry helps in calculating forces, vectors, and other quantities in physics, particularly in mechanics and wave studies.

Trigonometric Formulas in Applications

Some of the key formulas used in applications of trigonometry include:

  • Height and Distance Problems: Trigonometric ratios like sine, cosine, and tangent are used to calculate heights and distances that cannot be measured directly.
  • Law of Sines: a/sin(A) = b/sin(B) = c/sin(C) is used in non-right angled triangles to calculate missing sides or angles.
  • Law of Cosines: c² = a² + b² - 2ab cos(C) is used to find the length of a side or angle in a triangle when other sides and angles are known.

Conclusion

Trigonometry is a powerful tool that enables the solution of complex problems in various fields. By applying trigonometric formulas and understanding the relationships between angles and sides, we can solve real-life problems efficiently.
