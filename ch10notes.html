Notes - Chapter 10: Circles

Introduction to Circles

A circle is a set of all points in a plane that are at a fixed distance (radius) from a given point (center). The study of circles involves understanding various properties related to the radius, circumference, and area, as well as the angles and segments associated with them.

Key Concepts in Circles

  • Center: The fixed point from which every point on the circle is equidistant.
  • Radius: The distance from the center to any point on the circle.
  • Diameter: The longest chord of the circle, passing through the center, and is twice the length of the radius.
  • Circumference: The perimeter or boundary line of the circle. It is calculated as C = 2πr, where r is the radius.
  • Area: The space enclosed by the circle, calculated using the formula A = πr².

Important Theorems and Properties

  • The Angle at the Center Theorem: The angle subtended by a chord at the center of a circle is twice any angle subtended by the same chord at the circumference.
  • Chord Properties: Chords that are equidistant from the center of the circle are equal in length.
  • Inscribed Angle Theorem: The angle subtended by a chord at any point on the circumference is half of the central angle.
  • Secant and Tangent: A secant is a line that intersects the circle at two points, while a tangent touches the circle at exactly one point.

Applications of Circles

Circles have many real-life applications in various fields. Some examples include:

  • Engineering and Design: Circles are fundamental in the design of gears, wheels, and other rotational machinery.
  • Astronomy: The orbits of planets and satellites are often elliptical, which is closely related to circular motion.
  • Architecture: Circular designs are often used in domes, arches, and other structural elements.
  • Art and Design: Circles are often used in artwork and logo design due to their symmetry and aesthetic appeal.

Key Circle Formulas

  • Circumference: 
    C = 2πr
  • Area: 
    A = πr²
  • Length of an Arc: 
    Length = (θ / 360) × 2πr
    where θ is the central angle in degrees.
  • Area of a Sector: 
    Area = (θ / 360) × πr²
    where θ is the central angle in degrees.
