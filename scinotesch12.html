Notes - Chapter 12: Magnetic Effects of Electric Current

Introduction

The magnetic effect of electric current is the phenomenon where an electric current produces a magnetic field around it. This chapter explains the relationship between electricity and magnetism and their practical applications.

Magnetic Field and Field Lines

  • Magnetic Field: Region around a magnet or a current-carrying conductor where magnetic forces can be detected.
  • Magnetic Field Lines: Imaginary lines that represent the direction and strength of the magnetic field.
  • Field lines around a current-carrying straight wire are concentric circles.

Right-Hand Thumb Rule

  • Used to find the direction of the magnetic field around a current-carrying conductor.
  • Thumb points in the direction of current, fingers curl in the direction of magnetic field lines.

Magnetic Field Due to a Current in a Circular Loop

  • The magnetic field lines near the loop are similar to those of a bar magnet.
  • The direction of magnetic field can be found using the right-hand thumb rule for loops.

Force on a Current-Carrying Conductor in a Magnetic Field

  • A conductor carrying current experiences a force when placed in a magnetic field.
  • Direction of force is given by Flemingâ€™s Left-Hand Rule.
  • Applications: Electric motors, loudspeakers.

Electromagnet

  • When a soft iron core is placed inside a coil carrying current, it becomes magnetized.
  • Strength of electromagnet depends on current, number of turns, and core material.
  • Applications: Electric bells, relays, cranes.

Electric Motor

  • A device that converts electrical energy into mechanical energy using magnetic effects of current.
  • Based on the force experienced by a current-carrying conductor in a magnetic field.

Electromagnetic Induction

  • Process of producing induced current in a coil by changing magnetic field around it.
  • Principle behind generators and transformers.
