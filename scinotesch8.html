Notes - Chapter 8: Heredity

Introduction

Heredity is the biological process through which traits are passed from parents to their offspring. It explains why offspring resemble their parents and helps us understand the variations within a species.

Key Concepts in Heredity

  • Genes: Units of heredity located on chromosomes that determine traits.
  • Alleles: Different forms of a gene that determine variations in traits.
  • Dominant and Recessive Traits: Dominant alleles mask recessive ones in determining traits.
  • Genotype and Phenotype: Genotype is the genetic makeup; phenotype is the observable trait.

Mendelian Genetics

  • Gregor Mendel's experiments with pea plants laid the foundation of heredity.
  • Principles of segregation and independent assortment explain how traits are inherited.

Variation and Mutation

  • Variations arise due to differences in genes and alleles.
  • Mutations are changes in DNA that can lead to new traits.

Importance of Heredity

  • Helps understand genetic diseases.
  • Used in selective breeding and genetics research.
  • Explains evolutionary processes.
