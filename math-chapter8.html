Notes - Chapter 8: Introduction to Trigonometry

Introduction to Trigonometry

Trigonometry is a branch of mathematics that studies the relationships between the sides and angles of triangles. It is widely used in various fields, including physics, engineering, and astronomy. Trigonometry focuses on the trigonometric ratios such as sine, cosine, and tangent, which help in calculating unknown angles and sides in right-angled triangles.

Basic Trigonometric Ratios

The three basic trigonometric ratios are:

  • Sine (sin): The ratio of the length of the opposite side to the hypotenuse.
    sin(θ) = opposite / hypotenuse
  • Cosine (cos): The ratio of the length of the adjacent side to the hypotenuse.
    cos(θ) = adjacent / hypotenuse
  • Tangent (tan): The ratio of the length of the opposite side to the adjacent side.
    tan(θ) = opposite / adjacent

Trigonometric Functions for Other Angles

Trigonometric functions are not limited to right-angled triangles. They can also be applied to any angle in the unit circle. For angles between 0° and 360°, the sine, cosine, and tangent functions help determine the coordinates and relationships between points on the circle.

Trigonometric Identities

There are several key identities used in trigonometry:

  • Pythagorean Identity:
    sin²(θ) + cos²(θ) = 1
  • Reciprocal Identities:
    csc(θ) = 1/sin(θ), sec(θ) = 1/cos(θ), cot(θ) = 1/tan(θ)
  • Angle Sum and Difference Identities:
    sin(A ± B) = sin(A)cos(B) ± cos(A)sin(B)

Applications of Trigonometry

Trigonometry has many real-world applications such as:

  • In navigation and mapping (e.g., triangulation to determine distances between two points).
  • In physics (e.g., to calculate forces acting on an object).
  • In engineering (e.g., designing structures like bridges and buildings using angles and forces).
  • In computer graphics and animation (e.g., calculating angles and distances in 3D space).

Examples and Applications

Consider a right-angled triangle with an angle of 30° and a hypotenuse of 10 units. To find the length of the opposite side, use the sine function: 

sin(30°) = opposite / 10

Thus, opposite = 10 * sin(30°) = 10 * 0.5 = 5 units.

Important Definitions

- Sine (sin): Ratio of the opposite side to the hypotenuse in a right-angled triangle.
- Cosine (cos): Ratio of the adjacent side to the hypotenuse in a right-angled triangle.
- Tangent (tan): Ratio of the opposite side to the adjacent side in a right-angled triangle.
- Hypotenuse: The longest side of a right-angled triangle, opposite the right angle.
