Notes - Chapter 12: Areas Related to Circles

Introduction to Areas Related to Circles

This chapter focuses on understanding the areas of various segments and sectors of a circle. We explore how to calculate the area of a circle, a sector, and a segment, as well as how these concepts apply to real-life scenarios.

Key Concepts in Areas Related to Circles

  • Area of a Circle: The area enclosed by the boundary of the circle. The formula for the area is A = πr², where r is the radius of the circle.
  • Area of a Sector: A sector is a part of the circle. The area of a sector with a central angle θ (in degrees) is given by A = (θ / 360) × πr².
  • Area of a Segment: A segment is the area bounded by a chord and the arc it subtends. The area of a segment is the area of the sector minus the area of the triangle formed by the radius and the chord.

Important Formulas

  • Area of a Circle: 
    A = πr²
  • Area of a Sector: 
    A = (θ / 360) × πr²
    (where θ is the central angle in degrees)
  • Area of a Segment: 
    Area of Segment = Area of Sector - Area of Triangle

Steps for Calculating the Area of a Sector

  1. Step 1: Identify the radius (r) and the central angle (θ) of the sector.
  2. Step 2: Apply the formula A = (θ / 360) × πr² to calculate the area of the sector.
  3. Step 3: For a segment, subtract the area of the triangle from the area of the sector to find the area of the segment.

Applications of Areas Related to Circles

The concepts of areas related to circles have various applications in real life:

  • Engineering: Calculating the area of circular components such as gears, wheels, and pulleys.
  • Astronomy: Calculating the area covered by the orbit of planets, moons, or satellites.
  • Architecture: Designing circular structures such as domes and arches.
  • Land Measurement: Estimating the area of circular plots or circular fields for farming and landscaping.

Real-Life Examples

  • Clock Faces: Calculating the area of a clock face to determine the amount of material needed for manufacturing.
  • Pizza Slices: Determining the area of a pizza slice (sector of a circle) to understand the amount of pizza one is consuming.
  • Rotating Wheels: Calculating the surface area covered by rotating wheels in machinery or vehicles.
