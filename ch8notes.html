Introduction to Trigonometry

Trigonometry is a branch of mathematics that studies relationships between the sides and angles of triangles. It has applications in various fields like physics, engineering, and architecture. The primary focus in trigonometry is to understand how angles relate to the lengths of sides in right-angled triangles.

Key Concepts in Trigonometry

  • Trigonometric Ratios: These ratios help in relating the angles of a right-angled triangle to the lengths of its sides. The primary ratios are:
    • Sine (sin) = Opposite / Hypotenuse
    • Cosine (cos) = Adjacent / Hypotenuse
    • Tangent (tan) = Opposite / Adjacent
  • Reciprocal Ratios: These are the reciprocal of the basic trigonometric ratios:
    • Cosecant (csc) = 1 / Sine
    • Secant (sec) = 1 / Cosine
    • Cotangent (cot) = 1 / Tangent
  • Unit Circle: A unit circle helps us understand the relationship between the angle and the coordinates of points on a circle with radius 1. It is particularly useful in defining trigonometric functions for all angles.

Applications of Trigonometry

Trigonometry is widely used in various fields such as:

  • Architecture: Designing buildings, bridges, and other structures with precise angles and measurements.
  • Astronomy: Determining distances and angles between celestial bodies.
  • Navigation: Calculating distances and angles between locations for navigation purposes, such as air travel and maritime navigation.
  • Physics: Analyzing the behavior of waves, forces, and motion.

Important Trigonometric Identities

Trigonometric identities are essential for simplifying and solving problems in trigonometry. Some of the key identities include:

  • Pythagorean Identity: 
    sin²θ + cos²θ = 1
  • Sum and Difference Identities: 
    sin(A ± B) = sinAcosB ± cosAsinB
    cos(A ± B) = cosAcosB ∓ sinAsinB
  • Double Angle Identities: 
    sin(2A) = 2sinAcosA
    cos(2A) = cos²A - sin²A
