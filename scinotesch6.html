Notes - Chapter 6: Control and Coordination

Introduction

Control and coordination are vital processes that help organisms respond to changes in their environment and regulate internal functions. This chapter explores how the nervous and endocrine systems coordinate these activities in animals.

Nervous System

  • Structure: Composed of the brain, spinal cord, and nerves.
  • Function: Transmits electrical impulses for quick responses.
  • Neurons: Specialized cells that carry signals.
  • Reflex Action: Involuntary and immediate response to stimuli.

Endocrine System

  • Glands: Produce hormones (chemical messengers).
  • Functions: Regulates long-term processes like growth, metabolism, and reproduction.
  • Examples: Pituitary gland, thyroid gland, adrenal gland.

Coordination in Plants

  • Plants respond to stimuli through growth movements called tropisms (e.g., phototropism, gravitropism).
  • Coordination is chemical, involving plant hormones like auxins.
