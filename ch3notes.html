Notes - Chapter 3: Pair of Linear Equations in Two Variables

Introduction to Pair of Linear Equations in Two Variables

A pair of linear equations in two variables is a system of two linear equations that involve two unknowns. The general form of these equations is:

ax + by = c and dx + ey = f

Where:

  • a, b, d, e are constants (coefficients of x and y).
  • x, y are the variables (unknowns).
  • c, f are constants.

Methods of Solving a Pair of Linear Equations

There are three main methods to solve a pair of linear equations in two variables:

  • Graphical Method: Plot both equations on a graph and find the point of intersection.
  • Substitution Method: Solve one equation for one variable and substitute it into the other equation.
  • Elimination Method: Add or subtract the equations to eliminate one of the variables.

Graphical Method

The graphical method involves plotting the equations on a graph. The point where both lines intersect is the solution to the system of equations.

Example: Solve the system of equations using the graphical method:

  • 2x + y = 5
  • x - y = 1
Plotting these on a graph, we find that the lines intersect at the point (3, 2). So, the solution is x = 3 and y = 2.

Substitution Method

In the substitution method, we solve one of the equations for one variable and substitute this expression into the other equation to find the second variable.

Example: Solve the system of equations:

  • 3x + 4y = 12
  • 2x - y = 4
Step 1: Solve the second equation for y:
y = 2x - 4.
Step 2: Substitute y = 2x - 4 into the first equation and solve for x, then substitute x to find y.

Elimination Method

The elimination method involves adding or subtracting the equations to eliminate one of the variables, and then solving for the remaining variable.

Example: Solve the system of equations:

  • 5x - 3y = 7
  • 3x + 4y = 11
Step 1: Multiply both equations by appropriate numbers so that the coefficients of one variable are the same (or negatives).
Step 2: Add or subtract the equations to eliminate that variable and solve for the remaining one.

Applications of Pair of Linear Equations in Two Variables

Pair of linear equations are used in real-life situations such as:

  • Determining the cost of goods based on their quantities.
  • Modeling relationships between two variables in economics.
  • Solving problems in geometry involving two unknowns.
