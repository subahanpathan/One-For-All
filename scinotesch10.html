Notes - Chapter 10: The Human Eye and the Colourful World

Introduction

The human eye is a remarkable organ that enables us to see and appreciate the colourful world around us. This chapter explores the structure and functioning of the eye and explains phenomena related to light and colour.

Structure of the Human Eye

  • Cornea: The transparent outer covering that helps focus light.
  • Iris and Pupil: Controls the amount of light entering the eye.
  • Lens: Focuses light onto the retina.
  • Retina: Contains photoreceptor cells that detect light and send signals to the brain.
  • Optic Nerve: Transmits visual information to the brain.

Common Eye Defects and Corrections

  • Myopia (Nearsightedness): Corrected using concave lenses.
  • Hypermetropia (Farsightedness): Corrected using convex lenses.
  • Presbyopia: Age-related difficulty in focusing on close objects.
  • Cataract: Clouding of the lens; treated surgically.

Colourful World

  • Dispersion of Light: Splitting of white light into its component colours using a prism.
  • Rainbow Formation: Caused by dispersion, reflection, and refraction of sunlight in raindrops.
  • Scattering of Light: Responsible for the blue colour of the sky and reddish appearance of the sun at sunrise and sunset.
